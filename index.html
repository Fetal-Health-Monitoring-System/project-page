A U-Net based approach to monitor fetal cephalic development for early detection of tumors and down syndrome based on the shape and size of fetal head

Abstract

In this project, we present a method for monitoring fetal brain development using ultrasound images. The approach relies on U-Net, a neural network known for image segmentation tasks. By analyzing ultrasound images, our model identifies and tracks critical features in the developing fetal brain. This method provides a way to monitor brain growth and detect abnormalities without needing invasive techniques. The use of U-Net allows for precise segmentation, improving accuracy in identifying regions of interest. Our approach aims to support doctors in making informed decisions during pregnancy, enhancing the quality of prenatal care.
